                  <section data-level="1" id="ref1">
                   <!--[PREMOD1]-->
                   <span class="marker PREMOD1 mod-inline">
                   </span>
                   <p class="topic-paragraph">
                    <strong>
                     <span id="ref1332730">
                     </span>
                     Planet of the Apes
                    </strong>
                    ,
                    <a class="md-crosslink autoxref" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/art/novel">
                     novel
                    </a>
                    by French writer
                    <span id="ref1332731">
                    </span>
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/biography/Pierre-Boulle">
                     Pierre Boulle
                    </a>
                    , first published in French in 1963 and published in the U.S. in English translation the same year. The novel inspired the
                    <span id="ref1332732">
                    </span>
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/topic/Planet-of-the-Apes-film-1968">
                     1968 film
                    </a>
                    of the same name, which itself led to four sequels and two television series.
                   </p>
                   <!--[MOD1]-->
                   <span class="marker MOD1 mod-inline">
                   </span>
                   <!--[PREMOD2]-->
                   <span class="marker PREMOD2 mod-inline">
                   </span>
                   <p class="topic-paragraph">
                    Boulle’s satirical novel opens with a space-faring couple finding a canister floating in space. The canister contains a manuscript giving an account of the journey of three Frenchmen to a planet in orbit around
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/place/Betelgeuse-star">
                     Betelgeuse
                    </a>
                    . The manuscript is written by one of the men, Ulysse, a journalist who accompanied Professor Antelle and the physician Arthur Levain on the voyage. They name the Earth-like planet Soror and are surprised to discover a group of apparent humans. The humans behave like wild animals, with no signs of
                    <a class="md-dictionary-link md-dictionary-tt-off mw" data-term="culture" data-type="MW" href="https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/culture">
                     culture
                    </a>
                    or civilization. Suddenly the pack of humans is attacked by a group of armed
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/animal/Gorilla-primate-genus">
                     gorillas
                    </a>
                    who are dressed as hunters. Arthur Levain is killed, and Antelle and Ulysse are captured. Antelle is caged in a zoo, where he becomes feral, but Ulysse is placed in a biological laboratory, where he is required to mate with Nova, a female human from the pack.
                   </p>
                   <!--[MOD2]-->
                   <span class="marker MOD2 mod-inline">
                   </span>
                   <!--[PREMOD3]-->
                   <span class="marker PREMOD3 mod-inline">
                   </span>
                   <p class="topic-paragraph">
                    Ulysse persuades the lab’s scientist, a
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/animal/chimpanzee">
                     chimpanzee
                    </a>
                    named Zira, that he is intelligent, and he learns the simian language. He is permitted to speak before a scientific congress and is then granted his freedom. He works with Zira’s
                    <a class="md-dictionary-link md-dictionary-tt-off eb" data-term="fiancé" data-type="EB" href="https://www.britannica.com/dictionary/fianc%C3%A9">
                     fiancé
                    </a>
                    , Cornelius, who discovers that the
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/animal/primate-mammal">
                     primates
                    </a>
                    learned their culture some 10,000 years earlier from humans, who had trained apes to serve them and gradually allowed the apes to supplant them. When Nova gives birth to a boy who is able to cry, the apes fear trouble from a possible race of intelligent humans and plan to eliminate Ulysse, Nova, and the baby. Zira and Cornelius help the family to get to a spaceship so that they can return to
                    <a class="md-crosslink" data-show-preview="true" href="https://www.britannica.com/place/Earth">
                     Earth
                    </a>
                    .
                   </p>
